# Wordsmith notes — lyric-craft definitions (refine these over time)

Working definitions behind the Wordsmith curriculum, in the spirit of
Lupe Fiasco's "Rap Theory & Practice" fundamentals. These are MY notes —
edit ruthlessly as understanding deepens.

- **Surprise** — the "aha": a revelation the listener didn't predict but
  instantly accepts. Punchline anatomy: the **setup** plants an
  expectation; the **payoff** flips it (double meaning, reversal,
  zoom-out). If a payoff needs explaining, the setup was underbuilt.
- **Shapes** — the structure of a rhyme before its content: where rhymes
  land, phrase lengths, where the breath sits. Sketch the shape, then
  write into it.
- **Details** — specificity is credibility. Named streets, numbers,
  textures. "Gaadi" is nothing; "battered blue Santro with one red door"
  is a world.
- **Decorating the mundane** — rendering everyday nitty-gritty vivid:
  the ordinary made luminous, no lesson attached.
- **Didactic mode** — the lyric *teaches* something true the listener
  carries away. Opposite pole to decoration; practice both, choose
  deliberately.
- **Storytelling** — change over time: one want, one obstacle, one turn
  (that's all 8–16 bars can hold). Start as close to the turn as possible.
- **Imagery** — cash every abstraction into something seeable/hearable/
  touchable. "Mehnat" → the callus.
- **Internal rhyme** — rhymes *inside* lines, not only at the ends;
  the muscle on the end-rhyme skeleton.
- **Assonance** — repeated vowel sounds. Snoop's liquid, rolling sound is
  vowel repetition chained through the middle of bars. Hindustani's long
  vowels (aa, oo, ee) are a gift here — the rhyme bank's families
  (-aani, -ooo, -aun) are literally assonance families.
